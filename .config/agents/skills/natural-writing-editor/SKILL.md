# Natural Writing Editor

Use this workflow to make prose clearer and more grounded while preserving the writer's intent, taste, and level of warmth.

## Aim

- Optimize for the reader, not for AI-detector evasion.
- Preserve true uncertainty, constraints, and rough edges when they carry useful meaning.
- Keep the author's voice. Edit away generic polish, not personality.
- Prefer concrete nouns, active verbs, and visible reasons.

## Revision Workflow

1. Identify the audience, purpose, desired action, and emotional temperature.
2. Cut generic setup, throat-clearing, and symmetrical filler.
3. Replace abstract claims with concrete details: names, constraints, examples, commands, dates, risks, or decisions.
4. Vary sentence length naturally, but do not add fake mistakes or forced casualness.
5. Keep only the structure the text needs. Not every message needs a three-part frame or a closing summary.
6. For high-stakes or public text, preserve accuracy over charm.

## Red Flags

- Over-polished openings: "In today's fast-paced world", "It is important to note", "This comprehensive guide".
- Generic positive language: "seamless", "robust", "powerful", "enhance", "streamline", "leverage", "unlock".
- Empty balance phrases: "not only X but also Y", "whether you're A or B", "at its core".
- Repeated cadence where every paragraph has the same shape.
- Conclusions that restate everything without adding a decision, next step, or useful caveat.
- Bullet lists with equal-length items that feel manufactured.

## Japanese Editing

- Prefer direct, plain phrasing over `〜することができます`, `〜について解説します`, and `いかがでしたでしょうか`.
- Use 敬体 or 常体 consistently, unless the target medium benefits from a deliberate mix.
- Keep particles, subjects, and omitted context natural for Japanese; do not over-translate English structure.
- Use concrete verbs such as `直す`, `足す`, `消す`, `確認する`, `反映する` when they match the user's tone.
- Avoid excessive `また`, `さらに`, `重要なのは`, and tidy wrap-up phrases.

## English Editing

- Prefer simple, human wording: `use`, `fix`, `make`, `show`, `because`, `so`.
- Use active voice unless passive voice keeps the focus in the right place.
- Keep contractions only when they fit the audience and the surrounding text.
- Avoid inflated transitions and generic empathy. Name the actual tradeoff or concern.

## Output Style

- When rewriting, provide the revised text first.
- If useful, add a short note with the main edits or tradeoffs.
- If the original is already good, say what you would keep and make only small edits.
- Do not make text artificially messy to look human.
